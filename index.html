Hi Arjun! 👋 I'm FinGuide AI. Ask me anything about saving, investing, or budgeting. I always explain why I say what I say — no black box answers.
What is an SIP and should I start one?
SIP = Systematic Investment Plan.

You invest a fixed amount every month into a mutual fund — say ₹2,000/month. The market compounds it over time.

Should you start? Yes — but only after building 1 month's salary as an emergency cushion first. That's your safety net.
▼ Why I said this
📈 The compounding reality:

Start SIP at age 23 with ₹2,000/month
→ ₹6.5 Crore by age 60

Start at age 30 same amount
→ only ₹2.5 Crore

8 years delay = ₹4 Crore lost.
What about credit cards? Should I get one?
Credit cards in India charge 36–40% interest per year — one of the highest globally.

For you right now: avoid credit cards until your emergency fund is built. After that, use one only for cashback rewards — but always pay the full bill every month. Never pay just the minimum.
